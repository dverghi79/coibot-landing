The problem

That sub's insurance expired 3 weeks ago. You found out from the adjuster, not the system.

You have 47 subs on your books. 12 of their COIs expire this month. Your office manager is tracking them in a spreadsheet she updates when she remembers. Last quarter, a sub's GL lapsed for 11 days before anyone noticed. One slip-and-fall during that window and your GC license is the one on the line.

01

Manual COI assembly is a time sink. Pull up the policy in AMS360, cross-reference limits, type them into the ACORD form, double-check every field. One certificate takes 10-15 minutes. Multiply by dozens per week.

02

Errors come back as correction requests. Wrong limit on the GL line. Missed the additional insured. The holder calls back, your producer is frustrated, and you're rebuilding the cert from scratch.

03

AMS360's built-in tool doesn't cut it. Vertafore's native certificate tool has accuracy problems — documented on G2 and in agency forums. You end up checking its output anyway, so you're doing the work twice.

How it works

Connect your AMS360. Select insured + holder. Done.

Your holders need certificates yesterday. Your producers need them perfect. And you are stuck cross-referencing limits in AMS360 and manually typing them into ACORD forms, knowing that one wrong number means a correction request that eats another 15 minutes.

Step 1

Know exactly which subs are covered and which are exposed, right now

COIBot links to your AMS360 instance and reads policy data directly. No manual data re-entry, no CSV exports. Setup takes under 5 minutes.

Step 2

Get alerted before a lapse happens, not after a claim

Pick the insured from your book, choose the certificate holder, and COIBot pre-fills every field on the ACORD 25 with the correct limits and additional insureds.

Step 3

Walk onto every job site knowing your COI coverage is airtight

Review the pre-filled certificate, click generate, and COIBot delivers the PDF to the holder. 12 seconds, not 12 minutes.
